IT PRACTICUM OVERVIEW

As a requirement for an IT degree in Malayan Colleges Laguna, we must undergo an IT Practicum that requires 486 credited hours to complete in the span of 12 weeks. We were given multiple options on how to conduct the IT Practicum and I chose the Accenture Technology Academy program which grants access to the AWS Educate platform where I would need to complete four specific modules that would generate 120 credited hours. Due to the large number of hours needed to complete the IT Practicum, I must take courses from another platform alongside the ATA program where I would get most of my credited hours. As per recommendation, I enrolled in LinkedIn Learning.

The AWS Educate platform requires students to read the provided material in order to answer chapter quizzes. Some of the courses also have hands-on activities where participants must follow a set of step-by-step instructions while navigating through a simulation of the AWS facilities to accomplish tasks. At the end of the course, there is a final assessment like the chapter quizzes in which the participant must answer multiple choice questions and must score a certain threshold in order to proceed. On the other hand, the LinkedIn Learning platform only requires the participant to go through the video sections of the course. The chapter quizzes between videos are optional. Some of the courses also offer exercise files for download so that you can test what you have learned but it is also optional.

AWS Educate

120 credited hours

On this platform, I had to complete four courses that focused on Amazon Web Services which gave an overview on the capabilities of the service as well as instructions on the basics of operating and navigating the system, later courses also explained the different technologies involved in the service.

SYNTHESIS

Before taking the IT practicum, we were presented with multiple paths we can take. I chose the ATA program for convenience and ease since it will put me in a difficult position if any problem arises during the span of 12 weeks. If the schedule was more lenient, I would prefer to find a company to train under. Seeing how professionals work is a valuable experience but with my limited skills now, I may not be able to fully take advantage of the opportunity. The path that I chose which focuses on online learning is not bad compared to the first option since the learning platforms used is excellent, all things considered.

For the first part of my IT practicum, AWS Educate, I think the platform itself has excellent tools for learning. Hands-on activities use the actual AWS system and not a stripped-down version of a simulation, this is important because compared to the activities we went through in our network courses which uses a simulated environment that is a basic version of the actual system and equipment. In terms of the four courses we were designated, I only appreciated the second course "Introduction to Cloud 101" because it is the only one with actual hands-on training, the last two courses were in a way, disappointing since it is only filled with reading material. For LinkedIn Learning, most, if not all the courses I took were only composed of videos, short quizzes, and exercise files. The quizzes and exercise files are entirely optional. The videos are well organized and are split into shorter topics which is good if you need to find specific info.

I have spent nearly four years in college in order to earn an IT degree and these four years have been full of ups and downs, most notably having to spend my last two years of college at home because of the pandemic situation. This IT practicum is supposed to be the culmination of those four years alongside our capstone project. Even though I included projects in my portfolio that I have been involved with, I don’t think they are worth showcasing because their scale is small, and the finished outputs are not impressive. This really made me realize that I must study different technologies and work on more projects in the future.
